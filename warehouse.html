Accountancy Enterprise Data Warehouse

A central, cloud-hosted data warehouse for mid-to-large UK accountancy practices — independent of practice management software, ready for reporting, automations and AI.

The problem with legacy data infrastructure

Many mid-to-large UK accountancy practices still run on-premises software backed by Microsoft SQL Server, with reporting built on a Microsoft-centric pipeline of Fabric and Power BI connected straight to operational databases. That model is under increasing pressure.

Common challenges include:

  • High cost of maintaining on-premises SQL Server environments
  • Capacity strain when operational databases are also used for analytics and reporting — risking outages
  • Difficulty extracting data for AI and large language models that need accurate business context

Few firms have implemented a modern enterprise data warehouse on a cloud platform. The sector is shifting toward cloud-hosted software such as Xero, which sits poorly with existing management reporting and automations. Over the next decade, that migration is expected to erode the dominance of the SQL Server, Fabric and Power BI ecosystem.

Other industries have already moved to modern cloud data platforms. Accountancy has lagged — despite heavy reliance on data-driven reporting for time and fees, client services and operational efficiency.

A central cloud data warehouse

Whetstone Data Engineering Ltd implements a central, cloud-hosted enterprise data warehouse for mid-to-large accountancy practices (typically 250+ employees). The warehouse is independent of any particular practice management or service line software. It ingests data from multiple sources, including:

  • Service line software used to deliver client work (for example payroll, audit, accounts production or insolvency)
  • Practice-wide software: practice management (time and fees), HR (applicants, business units, time off) and IT (user directories, document management)
  • Public sources such as Companies House and the Charity Commission

What the warehouse enables

Advanced reporting that many large practices cannot achieve today — constrained by legacy systems and limited data engineering resource — becomes practical. Examples include:

  • Workflow tracking and capacity planning — combining service line work with HR availability, business units and time off
  • Client payrolls due this week, combining payroll software and workflow data
  • Reporting on statutory deadlines from Companies House

These capabilities support service line efficiency, management decision-making and readiness for a more data-centric operating environment.

Automations can also synchronise data between systems to improve consistency and cut manual entry. For example, employee onboarding can take data from a job applications system, pre-populate HR records and flow through into the practice management system.

How delivery works

An experienced senior technical engineer with software and data expertise is deployed into the client practice for six to twelve months. They own the project and deliver the objectives with minimal oversight from client management.

  • Ownership: all data and code developed belongs exclusively to the client
  • Billing: charged on a transparent per-day rate
  • Governance: regular fortnightly or monthly presentation reports cover progress and invite input

Why engage Whetstone rather than building in-house

Compared with an internal implementation, this approach brings familiarity with the technology stack, prior implementations at other firms and modern cloud expertise that traditional practices often lack. Work can typically be completed at lower cost and on a faster timeline, while avoiding costly architectural mistakes — components and decisions have already been validated in an enterprise accountancy context.

After go-live

Once the warehouse is operational and feeding live management dashboards, ongoing maintenance is light: keeping pipelines healthy, adjusting for schema changes and handling model change requests. Training can equip the client’s internal team to take this on, with optional ongoing support (for example one day per month).

Project stages

Delivery follows structured stages, so progress is steady and visible:

  1. Environment setup Cloud platform, version control, CI/CD and dbt
  2. Pipeline foundation Airflow ingestion pipeline setup, Fivetran
  3. Source ingestion Practice management replication to BigQuery and core services via Airflow, then enrichment from peripheral sources
  4. Data modelling in dbt Staging models for standardisation, marts for reporting, a Kimball-style fact–dimension model and standard models for clients, staff and jobs
  5. Reporting and automations Collaboration with reporting and automation teams to migrate reports and build use cases
  6. Transition to maintenance Handover, training and steady-state support
